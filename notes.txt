Sure! Here's a breakdown of every field:

---

**`success`** — Just confirms the API call worked. `true` means the data was fetched successfully.

---

### 📍 `location`
- **`latitude` / `longitude`** — The exact GPS coordinates the forecast is for (near KNUST, Kumasi).

---

### 🌤️ `current` (right now)
- **`time`** — The timestamp of the current reading: May 14, 2026 at 4:00 PM.
- **`interval`** — Data is updated every **900 seconds (15 minutes)**, so readings are fairly real-time.
- **`temperature_2m`** — Actual air temperature at **2 metres above ground**: **25.6°C**.
- **`relative_humidity_2m`** — How much moisture is in the air at 2m height: **89%** — very humid.
- **`apparent_temperature`** — The **"feels like"** temperature accounting for humidity and wind: **29.7°C**. This is why it feels hotter than 25.6°C.
- **`precipitation`** — Rain that has fallen in the current interval: **0.3mm** — light drizzle.
- **`weather_code`** — A standard WMO numerical code for the weather condition. **55** = Dense Drizzle.
- **`weatherDescription`** — Human-readable version of the weather code: **"Dense Drizzle"**.

---

### 📅 `daily` (7-day forecast, May 14–20)
Each array index corresponds to a day in the `time` array — so index 0 = May 14, index 1 = May 15, and so on.

- **`temperature_2m_max`** — The **highest temperature** expected each day (e.g. 32.5°C on Thursday).
- **`temperature_2m_min`** — The **lowest temperature** expected each day (e.g. 24.2°C on Thursday, typically at night).
- **`precipitation_sum`** — **Total rainfall** expected for the day in millimetres. Saturday (20.5mm) is the heaviest — that's significant rain.
- **`precipitation_probability_max`** — The **peak chance of rain** during the day as a percentage. Thursday is at 100% — rain is essentially certain.
- **`relative_humidity_2m_max`** — The **highest humidity** level expected that day. Most days are hitting 96–100% — extremely humid.
- **`weather_code`** — WMO code for the day's expected condition (e.g. 95 = Thunderstorm, 81 = Rain Showers, 51 = Light Drizzle).
- **`weatherDescriptions`** — Human-readable version of each day's weather code.

---

### 🌿 `riskInsights`
This is your app's custom layer on top of the weather data — assessing farming risk per crop.

- **`crop`** — The crop being assessed (Maize, Cassava, Plantain).
- **`riskLevel`** — How risky current conditions are for that crop. All three are **High** right now.
- **`message`** — A specific advisory for the farmer (e.g. spray for fungal disease on maize).
- **`factors`** — The weather conditions driving the risk (high humidity + high rain probability).

---

### 📝 `overallSummary`
A plain English summary of the whole response — good for displaying directly to farmers who don't need to read all the numbers.
